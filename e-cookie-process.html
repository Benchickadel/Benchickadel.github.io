My son, Enzo, has been drawing a ton since we have been home and I wanted to convert one of his lettering drawings into a cookie cutter. I started by taking a pic with my phone.

In Photoshop

  1. changed to grey-scale
  2. adjusted levels
  3. added a Gaussian blur
  4. adjusted levels again to get a high contrast
  5. I tried to get a connected outline and remove some of the shading but not all. I was looking for a varied width outline. if you notice the upper left corner the 'E' is disconnected.  I fix that in the next step.

 

In Illustrator

  1. live traced
    1. removed any white/ignored white space
  2. Any place the outline was disconnected I would drag an anchor point to overlap another part of the outlined area and then Unite the overlapped area to eventually create a continuous area along the entire outside.
  3. I simplified the path to smooth out the look
  4. using the Direct select and handles adjustment tool I manually adjusted some of the areas that were too thin or the curves were not up to my liking!
  5. the outline pick on the left is to demonstrate what I wanted to see when I exported the dxf but the solid gave me an idea of what I wanted the final cookie to be like.
  6. I then ungrouped
  7. exported as a dxf

 

In Rhino

  1. reversed the original dxf to print it correctly
  2. offset all of the paths that I needed
    1. cutting extrusion paths (extruded 10mm)
    2. "grip" path - this is just a slightly wider ring on the cutting part of the model so you can grip it easier when removing or pressing down. (extruded 3 mm)
    3. I also wanted to create a texture layer that would mimic the shadow or thick boarder around the edge. (extruded 6mm)
  3. I used 'ChamferEdg' to angle the cutting edge to see if I could get a sharper cutting edge. On this model it worked.  I am still exploring the parameters and work flow to get it to work all the time.  The first time I tried it my model was smaller, only about 60mm from top to bottom of the 'E'. I then enlarged it and used  2mm cutting width offset and was able to get the chamfer to work without creating weird anomalies in the mesh.
  4. exported as an stl

 

3D printing

  1. imported into slicer
    1. used makerbot print 4.7
    2. printed on Method X
    3. PLA
  2. the chamfer worked. excited to try this out. cookie pics to come soon
