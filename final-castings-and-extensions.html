I first tested ice and I used the left over XTC-3D to test a cast as well.  This was done in an earlier mold.  What we noticed was that we liked the layer lines in these as they followed the ripples of the leaves.

This is just here to see the process of the Hawley retainer casting for reference.  Sealing and setting the wires that have been bent to fit the teeth plaster mold. Preparing the mold to cast the acrylic polymer. placing the powder and then the liquid.  After multiple layers have been built up it is ground and polished.

This is the mold with the layer marks and the orange is one that had the XTC-3D.  After polishing they both look nice but the rippled side looks better on the purple.

What's next...

I started to design a wire bending jig but so far that has proven more cumbersome than helpful and quickly bending the wire with tools is easier. I was trying to make it a consistent shape but it wasn't necessary.  If we add a "spine up the leaf I may make a simple wire crimper clamp.

 

The other tool my wife needs is a ridged mold to help when sanding.  Laying the pieces on a belt sander isn't easy because they are thin but a simple reverse leaf with a handle might work. Here's a start...
