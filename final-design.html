Getting started:  I tried a few different possible designs but I couldn't get a look that was simple but looked interesting while just working in Rhino. I searched for creating a venation pattern that would generate on the surface of a convex leaf shape but just couldn't get the "look" right. I moved to illustrator to see if that would help,  I'm a little more comfortable "sketching" in illustrator.  These patterns just weren't working. Detail was also going to be an issue as this couldn't be very large.  The leaf was only about 50mm in length overall so I tried a similar approach to what I used with the lamp clamp I created earlier.

I used Loft to create the rippled surface from two curves.  I mirrored the surface and then used FlowAlongSur to follow the leaf shape curves.  The important step here is the initial curves I used to create the leaf shape had to be Lofted not SurfaceFromPlanerCurves. This constrained the rippled surface to the shape of the leaf surface and I was able to get the angled effect. Instead of using a convex surface I opted for a flatter surface since the final product was only going to be 2-5mm thick.

This was the initial mold form but later I changed it to a bit more narrow and longer shape as per requested by my wife.

I had a final few alterations once I printed a few to show my wife.  The wire placement space was widened and the plaster mold was out because with walls the acrylic is too hard to remove unless I angled the walls.  But the Silicon would work great and then we could just cast more molds when needed.  So after printing a few iterations and testing in silicon I was ready to cast final test molds.
