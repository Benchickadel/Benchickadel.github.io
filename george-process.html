So...well..yeah, it's curious george. My boys have always loved george and the PBS show was really great because they always had an activity to try at home and it was filmed with real kids...anyway. My wife has always wanted a cookie cutter of george for my kids. I thought it would be a good exercise in figuring out the necessary parts to keep to preserve the image integrity. I did a quick google search and here's what I did...

In Photoshop

  1. changed to grey-scale
  2. cut-off his body (I only wanted the head)
  3. adjusted levels
  4. used the magic wand tool to select and delete all greys and keep just black.
  5. adjusted levels again to get a high contrast

In Illustrator

  1. This was pretty simple
  2. live traced
    1. removed any white/ignored white space
  3. ungrouped
  4. exported as dxf

In Rhino

  1. offset the outer most curve 2mm to create the cutting edge
  2. offset that curve to create a 'grip' or wider edge
  3. extruded cutting edge 10mm
  4. extruded grip 3mm This I made the entire back of the cutter
  5. extruded details 6mm to make impression into the dough.
  6. used boolean union to create one surface.
  7. I then drew curves under the nose, and forehead area to create cutouts in the cutter
  8. I extruded the curves and used boolean subtract to create the cutouts.
    1. the cutouts are intended to serve as a place to push the cookie dough out of the mold if it gets stuck.
  9. I tried to chamfer the cutting edge but kept running into trouble around the hair wisps.  I get getting strange anomalies and mesh holes.

3D printing

  1. imported into slicer
    1. used CURA 4.5
    2. printed on Ender pro 3
    3. PLA
