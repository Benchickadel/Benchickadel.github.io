3d scanning

I've had this Structure 3D scanner from work and have not really used it.  I thought this would be a good opportunity to generate some meshes to see if I could repair them. I told my kids that I read somewhere that the scanner works better with a pale flat(not shiny) surface so I would have to put flour all over them! HA!  I don't think it helped and it wasn't consistent but super fun embracing the messy side and getting them to take showers afterwards! I do think the idea is that the flour knocks out the shiny reflections that can effect the scanner and would work if I was more consistent or using a smaller object.  The interface is a little strange and there are a few different apps to use to calibrate and scan. I have the original scanner but a 2nd gen is out that I think is better.  Here's a pic of the scanner that fits onto an iPad. Once you start scanning you move around the object and pause occasionally for the software to do its thing and try to get as much of the undercuts and hidden areas as possible.  Also important for your objects to remain still!!!!

Importing to Rhino

The structure scanner app creates a .obj file that you email to yourself (other apps have different ways of transferring files but this seemed fairly simple).  I imported the .obj file into Rhino and it was a mess but I was surprised by the detail. There were totally open, goofy holes in places. It was super tiny (width was  0.42mm, couldn't figure out how to adjust that).  The first modeled I imported had over 150,000 poly count.  I was surprised that my computer didn't lag while scaling and editing.  After monkeying around with repair mesh, fill all holes, patch and such I ended up just turning on points and deleting a majority of the lower part of the model.  I reduced the poly count by 99% as well.  I kept trying to boolean mesh a box or cylinder to the models but nothing would work until I reduced the poly count.  My big friend was fill mesh hole. I had to do lots of deleting points because there were surfaces crossing into each other creating the mesh to struggle with boolean union or filling holes.

Finishing and exporting as .stl

I would like to say this was easy but I didn't actually get the models to truly union.  I could boolean difference both models with mesh primitives simultaneously and add mesh primitives to both but couldn't actually get them both to union together. Ended up making some dual Max Headroom pencil holder thingy. Wasn't sure how to fix the naked edges on one model (need to re-watch the videos again). I think thats where some of the union problems were happening.  I exported them as one stl and Cura didn't really flinch, seemed to think they were both together. I printed in standard mode and thought I added supports but the preview didn't show any so I gave it a shot. The preview also showed both models as one and there wasn't any strange interior printing. I'm interested in trying the structure more and seeing how to create better details (higher poly meshes) and still be able to work with obj files created.
