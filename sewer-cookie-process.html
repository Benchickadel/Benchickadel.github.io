Went for a walk the other day and noticed all of the maintenance covers on the street. We have been walking in the middle of more residential streets during this social distancing times and just noticed the street features more. I liked the textures and patterns on the covers and thought it would be a fun cookie, especially the sewer cover. I have a " water" and "drain" cookie cutter  in the works.

In Photoshop

  1. changed to grey-scale
  2. adjusted levels
  3. added a Gaussian blur
  4. adjusted levels again to get a high contrast
  5. I liked the "spooky" or "noise" in the picture and was trying to keep that to transfer to the cutter.

In Illustrator

  1. live traced
    1. removed any white/ignored white space
  2. removed any details/paths I thought would be too small to show up in cookie dough using the direct select tool
  3. ungrouped
  4. exported as a dxf

In Rhino

  1. reversed the original dxf to print it correctly
  2. offset all of the paths that I needed
    1. cutting extrusion paths
    2. "grip" path - this is just a slightly wider ring on the cutting part of the model so you can grip it easier when removing or pressing down.
  3. I created a press/stamp of the details in the center.  I did this by selecting the inner most ring and all the detail in side the ring and extruded that.  this would allow the cookie to have a raised detail in the surface where the letters and pattern details are located, mimicking the actual maintenance cover.
  4. extruded the cutting ring and grip ring.
    1. made sure to boolean union these parts but not include the press/print part of the cutter.
  5. I left 1mm of space around the entire press and ring cutter so I could maximize the area on the build plate when printing but still have two separate parts.
  6. exported as an stl

3D printing

  1. imported into slicer
    1. used makerbot print 4.7
    2. printed on Method X
    3. PLA
